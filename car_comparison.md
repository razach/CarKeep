# Car Ownership Analysis: RDX vs. BMW iX vs. Polestar 3
 
## Executive Summary & Recommendation

After rigorous review—including standardizing loan terms for an apples-to-apples comparison between the two CPO BMW iX options—the conclusion remains solid: **Keeping the Acura RDX is the financial winner.**

The new **2024 BMW iX Fairfax** scenario represents a 3-year total cost of **+$32,924** compared to keeping the RDX. By equalizing the loan terms (72 months at 6.19% APR), we see that the Fairfax iX is only $1,302 more expensive than the originally modeled CPO iX over 3 years, which directly corresponds to the ~$1,000 difference in their sticker prices ($47,999 vs $46,995).

**3-Year Total Cost Difference (vs. Keeping RDX):**
*   **Keep Acura RDX:** Baseline ($0)
*   **Buy BMW iX (Original CPO):** **+$31,622**
*   **Buy 2024 BMW iX (Fairfax CPO):** **+$32,924**
*   **Lease Polestar 3:** **+$33,275**
*   **Lease BMW iX (New):** **+$54,455**

**Recommendation:**
1.  **Financial Choice:** Keep the RDX. You save up to **$33,000+**.
2.  **New Car Choice (Best Value):** Both the **BMW iX Purchase (CPO)** and the **2024 BMW iX Fairfax** are functionally identical financial choices, providing the best value. The slight difference is purely based on the $1,000 gap in asking price. The **Polestar 3 Lease** remains a strong alternative.
3.  **Avoid:** The **BMW iX Lease**. It provides no equity, has high cash flow, and results in a **$54k** total 3-year cost difference.

## 3. The "Gotcha" on the Dealer's Estimated Payments
The worksheet shows a **$654/mo** estimated payment for 72 months (assuming $0 extra cash down, rolling in only the trade-in equity).

If we run the math backward on a $36,145.11 loan balance over 72 months equating to a $654 payment, **the dealership is assuming a staggering 9.14% APR** for these estimates. This is a classic sales sheet tactic.

---

## 4. True Monthly Payments (at Promotional 1.99% APR)
Because you qualify for the BMW CPO 1.99% promotional rate, your actual payments will be drastically lower than what is printed on this sheet. 

Here are the true monthly costs for both strategies over **72 months**:

### Scenario A: Trade-In Included
You give the dealer your RDX, they pay off your $5,000 Acura loan, and apply the remaining $14,100 equity directly to the BMW as a down payment.
* **Financed Amount:** $36,145.11
* **True APR:** 1.99%
* **Term:** 72 Months
* **Actual Monthly Payment:** **$533.00**
*(The dealer quoted $654; getting the 1.99% rate saves you $121/month just in interest).*

### Scenario B: Keep Trade-In Cash (The Recommended Strategy)
You give the dealer your RDX, they pay off your $5,000 Acura loan, but instead of applying the $14,100 equity to the new loan, they cut you a physical check for $14,100 which you can invest. You finance the entire cost of the BMW.
* **Financed Amount:** $50,245.11
* **True APR:** 1.99%
* **Term:** 72 Months
* **Actual Monthly Payment:** **$740.92** 

**Conclusion:** 
Do not be alarmed by the $654/mo or $753/mo payment estimates on the paper; the dealership's software is running at a 9.14% default interest rate. Ensure the F&I (Finance) manager inputs the 1.99% promotional rate before you sign, which drops the expected payment down to **$533/mo** (with trade) or **$740/mo** (without trade).

## Strategic Financial Decisions

### 1. The "Sweet Spot" Mileage Selection
During our Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) analysis, we analyzed three tiers of CPO vehicles (Low, Mid, and High Mileage). We concluded that the Mid-Mileage option at ~$47k and ~39k miles is the optimal financial sweet spot.

* **Warranty Breakdown:** 
    * It provides approximately 11,000 remaining miles of the original 4-year/50k factory bumper-to-bumper warranty, which should comfortably cover **Year 1** of ownership.
    * Once that expires, the vehicle is protected by 1 year of unlimited-mileage BMW CPO warranty, which covers **Year 2**.
    * Only **Year 3** goes without comprehensive warranty coverage (though the EV battery warranty remains active for 8 years/100,000 miles).
* **The Math:** Because it balances a low upfront purchase price with enough built-in warranty coverage to avoid buying an expensive $5,000 extended warranty, its true cost is mathematically superior. It perfectly balances risk and depreciation.
